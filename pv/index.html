Homemade Lentil & Vegetable Soup

High-fiber, no added sugar, and built around everyday ingredients.

Quick Recipe Overview

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cook: 35–40 minutes
  • Makes: 6 servings (adjust as needed)
  • Notes: Naturally dairy-free and gluten-free (check broth label)

Ingredients

Base

  • 1 tbsp olive oil (or avocado oil)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced (optional)
  • 1 cup dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (no added sugar if possible)
  • 6 cups low-sodium broth + water as needed
  • Cumin, smoked paprika (optional), bay leaf, black pepper

Finish

  • 2 cups chopped spinach or kale
  • 1–2 tbsp lemon juice or apple cider vinegar
  • Salt to taste (add slowly)

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Sauté onion, carrots, and celery 6–8 minutes. Add garlic 30 seconds.
  2. Add bell pepper (and zucchini). Stir in spices.
  3. Add lentils, tomatoes, broth, bay leaf. Simmer gently.
  4. Cook 25–30 minutes until lentils are tender. Add water if needed.
  5. Stir in greens 2–3 minutes. Finish with lemon/vinegar. Adjust salt.
  6. Serve and store leftovers safely.

Portion & Pairing Tips

  • Start with a measured bowl the first time, then adjust based on your needs.
  • Balance your meal: veggies + protein + intentional carb portions (if included).
  • If you use insulin or certain medications, discuss major eating changes with your clinician.

Carb Basics (Plain English)

Carbohydrates include starches, sugars, and fiber. Many people managing diabetes pay attention to carbs because carbs can affect blood glucose.

  • “No added sugar” does not mean “no carbs.”
  • Fiber can help meals feel more filling.
  • People respond differently. Portions and context matter.

FAQ

Will this soup “lower blood sugar”? No single recipe can promise that. This is simply a balanced, high-fiber meal idea.

Is it safe for everyone? Needs vary, especially with kidney disease or other conditions. Personalize with professional guidance if needed.

What about “miracle” diabetes products? Be cautious with cure/treatment claims. If it sounds too good to be true, it usually is.
